What is the cost of goods manufactured?
The cost of goods manufactured (COGM) is a schedule that tells the total production costs of a company in a specific time period.
How to calculate cost of goods manufactured?
To calculate cost of goods manufactured you should use the following formula:
cost of goods manufactured = beginning work in process inventory + total manufacturing cost - ending work in process inventory
Example: Company x start ht eyear with $17,000, during the year the factory spends $40,000 on direct materials and $15,000 on manufacturing overhead, in the end of the year the company has $20,000 in ending work in process inventory, what it the cost of goods manufactured?
First we should calculate the total manufacturing cost:
total manufacturing cost = direct materials + manufacturing overhead
total manufacturing cost = $40,000 + $15,000
total manufacturing cost = $55,000
Now let's calculate the cost of goods manufactured:
cost of goods manufactured = beginning work in process inventory + total manufacturing cost - ending work in process inventory
cost of goods manufactured = $17,000 + $55,000 - $20,000
cost of goods manufactured = $52,000
Why use cost of goods manufactured?
The cost of goods shold is usually used by management to get an idea of the overall production costs in order to decide if these costs are high or low.
